MANTIS AI Platform Landscapes

AI / Computational Biology

As part of the MIT AI Mantis Rising Scholar Program, I worked on creating and improving the MANTIS AI platform's landscapes for drug discovery, molecular dynamics, and patient trajectory analysis. This project involved developing computational models that can predict drug interactions and optimize treatment pathways.

The visualization shown here represents a multi-dimensional landscape of potential drug compounds, with color gradients indicating efficacy against specific targets. This work has implications for accelerating the drug discovery process and personalizing treatment approaches.

Brain Network Visualization

Neuroscience / Data Visualization

This visualization represents functional brain networks involved in mathematical and linguistic processing, based on fMRI data from the Human Connectome Project. Using graph theory and dynamic functional connectivity analysis, I mapped how different brain regions interact during specific cognitive tasks.

The network visualization shows nodes (brain regions) and edges (functional connections), with color coding indicating the strength of connections and the involvement of regions in different types of processing. This work contributes to our understanding of how the brain organizes itself for different cognitive functions.

Date:
July 2024
Role:
Lead Researcher
Technologies:
Python, NetworkX, Matplotlib, fMRI Analysis
